Strategy & Hypotheses

Strategy

Win the next AI unicorns early by packaging Wonder’s curated kitchens with Grubhub’s B2B rails (Corporate + Campus) into a Credits + Programmatic Meals offer that becomes the default food OS for SF startup offices (5–6 days/week). Concentrate service in SoMa, Mission Bay, and Hayes Valley so a single courier loop can hit 3–4 startups in under 8 minutes, boosting OTD without extra hardware.

Hypotheses

  • Recurring credits + predictable waves produce a step‑change in DEOL and AOV vs one‑off orders.
  • High startup density enables front‑desk drop‑offs—lockers optional—while still sustaining ≥95% OTD thanks to short loops and QR/photo proof.
  • “Better‑for‑you” bundles (protein bowls, hydration, late‑night soup) keep long‑hour teams productive and drive repeat.

Why Now (Signals)

  • 996‑style workweeks in SF: Ramp shows SF tech workers’ Saturday activity is up — 6‑day cadence is real.

  • Density advantage: 50+ AI startups within a 1‑mile spine (Mission Bay → Hayes) let us batch drop‑offs per block and hold OTD.
  • Distribution tailwind: Order Grubhub directly on Amazon; Prime includes ongoing Grubhub+.
  • Playbooks exist: Campus “Just Walk Out” & reusable packaging prove frictionless pickup + sustainability in dense venues.

Pilot & Success (SF AI — 5–6 days/week)

Scope

  • Sites: 10–20 AI startups clustered within a 1‑mile SF corridor (Seed→Series B), 120–250 employees.
  • Programs: 2 lunches + 1 late‑night dinner weekly with direct lobby or drop‑zone delivery; lockers only where buildings already demand them.
  • Offer: Tiered credits; policy rules via Grubhub Corporate; Wonder “better‑for‑you” bundles plus hydration/late‑night add‑ons.

Success Criteria (edit in PRD → KPIs)

  • Participation ≥ 60%; OTD ≥ 95%; Repeat‑30 ≥ 35%
  • NPS ≥ 70; Refund rate ≤ 1.5%
  • Hit weekly GFS forecast ±20% by Week 4; ≥ Base by Week 8

Gates

  1. Week 4 — Adoption: Credits must convert to ≥50% participation and ≥93% OTD per cluster, proving dense drop loops work.
  2. Week 8 — Habit: Participation ≥60%, OTD ≥95%, GFS on plan; greenlight scale kit if clusters stay within ±2 minutes of planned dwell.
  3. Week 12 — Scale: KPIs hold + refund rate ≤1.5% → expand to next 50 startups; open self‑serve funnel.

Mental model: Gate 1 validates adoption (credits + routing), Gate 2 locks habit (behavior + reliability), Gate 3 confirms the unit model so we can replicate the corridor playbook elsewhere.

Operating Mechanisms

  • Daily Ops: Plan cluster loops, track OTD, drop photo compliance, and desk dwell; fix‑forward each morning.
  • 2× Weekly: Experiment readouts on menu depth, wave spacing, and subsidy caps by cluster.
  • Weekly Exec: Path‑to‑Green dashboard; decisions in‑room (scale | fix | pause).

Risks & Mitigations

  • Lobby access fatigue → pre‑register couriers, share manifests with building ops, rotate drop zones.
  • Menu quality in batches → “batchable winners”; holding windows; rotate SKUs.
  • Policy edge cases → opinionated templates; audit log; manual override path.

Strategy PRD (MVP)

Problem

AI startups in SF run 5–6 day on‑site cadence with long hours. Admins want simple controls and predictable spend; teams want fast, nutritious food with zero friction.

Goals (KPIs)

  • GFS lift at pilot sites
  • DEOL +50–100% vs baseline; AOV +10–15%
  • OTD ≥95%; Repeat‑30 ≥35%; NPS ≥70

Users & JTBD

  • Employee: “Feed me well, fast, reliably between builds/sprints.”
  • Admin/Finance: “Policy‑compliant meals without overhead or waste.”
  • Kitchen/Ops: “Predictable waves, curated SKUs, minimal queueing.”

Assumptions

  • Campus + Corporate identity/wallet integration is feasible.
  • Dense corridors allow secure lobby or drop‑zone delivery; lockers only needed for specific compliance sites.
  • Top 15–20 items can be batched without NPS decay.

Scope (MVP)

  • Programmatic Meals: Recurrence (weekly), attendee list upload, per‑order & weekly caps, scheduled pickup windows.
  • Last‑100‑feet: Cluster drop‑offs with QR + photo verification; lightweight signage kit; lockers optional.
  • Unified Wallet + Policy: One wallet; 3 rules (time window, per‑order cap, weekly cap); audit log.
  • Predictive Menu & Batching v0: 20 curated SKUs; batch windows; stock‑level display; kitchen timers.

Non‑Goals

  • Custom budgets per micro‑team in MVP
  • Complex approval workflows
  • Full catering with bespoke menus per site

Acceptance

  • Create a program in ≤3 minutes; attach rate ≥15% of site orders by Week 4.
  • Median pickup dwell ≤90s; refunds ≤1.5%.
  • Wallet linkage success ≥98%; policy violations <1% of orders.

PRD FAQ

How is this different from regular delivery or catering?

We package credits + policy rails (Grubhub Corporate) with predictive batching and standardized pickup. It’s recurring, reliable, and fast during peaks — built for 5–6 day office cadence.

Do we even need lockers?

No. The default flow is direct lobby or drop‑zone delivery inside dense startup clusters. Couriers photo/QR verify each drop, and because loops cover 3–4 offices within 8 minutes, OTD stays high. Lockers are optional for buildings that already have them or mandate unattended storage.

Why focus on “healthy by default”?

Long‑hour teams need performance food. Curated, nutrition‑forward bundles raise satisfaction and repeat while controlling prep complexity.

How do credits work?

Use cloud‑style tiers (Seed/A/B). Credits flow through Corporate accounts with hard caps and time windows. This drives activation while keeping spend predictable.

How do we measure success quickly?

We run an adoption → habit → scale model: Week 4 proves credits + dense routes produce ≥50% participation, Week 8 locks habit with ≥60% participation and ≥95% OTD, Week 12 confirms unit economics so we can add 50 more startups. Miss a gate? Narrow menu + route complexity, then re-test.
